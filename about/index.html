A little bit (more) about me

Life before coding (I know, crazy to think about, right?)

I never once thought that I would enter the world of STEM. I have always tended toward humanities classes and thought that, while I had a wide variety of interests, that I would be more of the "artist" type above all else.

How it Started

It all started with a TV chef who I watched religiously throughout high school. Her name is Rachel Khoo, and she truly embodied everything I wanted from life: she worked out of her tiny, Parisian apartment, wore colorful, vintage clothes, and illustrated her life with delicate watercolor.

Then, one day, she began talking about coding. She said that she owed a large portion of her success to a website called "Skillcrush" and that this was the foundation of her web-design career. Her website began to change, looking far more professional than her blog had been previously.

After hearing about Rachel Khoo's coding endeavors, there came a massive uptick in STEM-related advertising‐ Karlie Kloss started a coding school for girls (see Code with Klossy) and suddenly it seemed like girls really could make a place for themselves in tech.With that being said though, I did not take to coding the first time around. I thought it was super fun, but I did not have the energy to commit to coding full-time (especially since I was already a student).

How it's going

Coding has truly changed my life. I'm able to decide my own work schedule and spend more time with my family, which is the reason I took it up in the first place! I no longer owe every moment of my productive time to an employer, but rather to myself.
